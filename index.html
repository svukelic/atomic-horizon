Why play this game instead of the others?

Because you want to feel unstoppable until you're not.
You drop into the fight in a customizable exosuit — 1000 kilograms of machine guns, heavy-duty explosives and reinforced steel. You're cutting through infantry like tissue paper, laying waste to enemy positions... until their heavy armor rolls in. Then you're scrambling, bleeding coolant, and praying your squad can cover your escape.

Unless... you planned for it. You prepared for it — and you watch as your squad rolls in with counter-fire, turning the ambush into a killing field.
That moment when overwhelming power meets overwhelming odds?

That's the game.
Unlike Warhammer 40k or Battletech:
  • Your suit IS your character — not a disposable unit. It's your legend. You name it. You wreck it. You rebuild it scarred and meaner.
  • Fast, brutal, decisive — no codex bloat, no hour-long turns. Drop in. Fight. Extract or die trying.
  • Squad or die — you're powerful alone, but the game WILL kill you for trying. Survival means tight coordination and covering fire while your reactor screams.
  • The operator power fantasy — you're Rambo, Schwarzenegger, and John Wick in an Iron Man suit — a one-man army… faced with another army. The challenge is taking down hordes of grunts, fortified military compounds, and massive war machines.
Every mission writes itself in fire and wreckage.

Every choice — your loadout, your tactics, your last stand — is yours.
Core mechanical differences:
Suit customization is the progression system You build and modify a specific exosuit over campaigns, not unlock new units or armies. Your gear choices have consequences in every scenario.
Quick turn resolution Turns are streamlined and move fast, keeping both players engaged without long downtime between actions.
Lethality is high, durability is tactical Cover, positioning, and squad support matter more than stat blocks. A well-placed shot can cripple even heavy units.
Asymmetric scenarios by default Missions are built around objectives and survival, not symmetric army-vs-army battles. You're usually outnumbered.
Straightforward card-based rules Mechanics are handled through cards, keeping gameplay clear and accessible without complex rulebook lookups or supplemental codices.
Mission-driven narrative progression Scenarios link into ongoing narratives where your choices, victories, and losses shape future missions and available resources.
